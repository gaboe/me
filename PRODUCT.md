# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site serves consulting clients, recruiters, and founders evaluating Gabriel Ecegi as a senior technical partner. Consulting clients need to understand whether he can bring order to unclear product and engineering problems. Recruiters need a reliable seniority signal and a clear career record. Founders need to see technical judgment, ownership, and the ability to translate business goals into durable software decisions.

## Product Purpose

This is a personal career and consulting website. Its purpose is to present Gabriel as a principal software consultant and technical leader with long-term hands-on engineering experience, consulting delivery, architecture ownership, and past CTO experience. Success means the right visitor can quickly validate credibility, understand the type of technical partnership offered, and make contact with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Technical, strict, and systematic. The brand should feel like an internal operating system for identification, classification, and monitoring: controlled brutalism, retro terminal, military logistics, and sci-fi HUD. It should communicate precision, seniority, operational discipline, and practical authority without becoming decorative or theatrical.

## Anti-references

Avoid neon cyberpunk, colorful gradients, glossy 3D effects, game-like sci-fi interfaces, modern SaaS minimalism, pastel palettes, soft rounded components, startup hype, crypto/AI hype aesthetics, recruiter-template portfolio design, and generic corporate executive bio styling. The site should not feel playful, ornamental, overly smooth, or like a marketing template.

## Design Principles

Identify before persuading: the interface should first establish who Gabriel is, what role he plays, and why the record is credible.

System over decoration: visual elements should feel like labels, registers, telemetry, documentation, or classification tools, not embellishment.

Seniority through restraint: authority should come from precise hierarchy, controlled language, and disciplined composition rather than loud claims.

Operational clarity: every section should reduce uncertainty for clients, recruiters, and founders by making experience, status, and contact paths explicit.

Archived roles stay precise: past CTO and venture work should be framed as important leadership experience without implying that closed roles are current.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for accessible contrast, readable text hierarchy, keyboard-friendly navigation, and semantic structure. Motion, scanlines, grain, and HUD effects should remain subtle enough not to interfere with legibility, and should respect reduced-motion preferences where animation is present.
